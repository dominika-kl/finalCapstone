Walking your dog in the UK

Enjoying the Great British countryside with your furry friend is a wonderful experience. There are lots of amazing places to visit, however it's really important to be mindful of other people, wildlife and farm animals - especially when you're out and about with your dog. It's always good to plan your route ahead, especially when you are in the area you are not familiar with. Check the weather and be prepared - seek out shades on hot days, bring plenty of water for you and for your dog. Don't forget snacks and poo bags! Most likely you will bump into other walkers with their pets. Be aware of the signs that either dog is scared or stressed out. When passing by livestock, always keep your dog on the lead. Now you are ready to go! Just a few more tips about the best locations for walking your dog. We had a chance to visit many places and the most beautiful and dog friendly spots were in: Wales, Cornwall and Scotland. Cornwall can be a bit busy, so if you are looking to relax in a quite environment, go north, up to Scotland. Isle of Skye, is amazing! Some places can be packed with tourists during high season, but if you choose to drive a bit further to the north, you won't be disappointed. You'll find fantastic views, wide, asny beaches and long trails.If you head west, Wales can offer you the spectacular views and hikes along the coastline.
